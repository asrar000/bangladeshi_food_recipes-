Shingara

Description

The shingara’s ancestor, the Samsa, originated in Central Asia before the 10th century. It was brought to the Indian subcontinent by Middle Eastern traders and chefs during the Delhi Sultanate (13th–14th century). While it began as a meat-filled snack for travelers, it evolved in Bengal into the potato-filled, flaky pastry we love today after the Portuguese introduced potatoes to the region in the 16th century.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour (maida)
  • Oil or ghee
  • Potatoes
  • Green peas
  • Ginger paste
  • Cumin seeds
  • Green chilies
  • Garam masala powder
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Prepare dough with flour, oil, salt and water
  2. Make potato and pea filling with spices
  3. Roll dough into circles and cut in half
  4. Shape into cones and fill with potato mixture
  5. Seal edges to form triangle shapes
  6. Deep fry until golden brown and crispy
  7. Serve hot with tamarind chutney
