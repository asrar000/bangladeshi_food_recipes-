Biriyani

Description

Biryani arrived in Bangladesh via the Mughals. In 1610, when Dhaka was declared the provincial capital, Mughal officials and subedars arrived from North India (particularly Lucknow) bringing their royal chefs with them. Over time, these royal recipes blended with local Bengali tastes—such as using mustard oil in the famous Hajir Biryani or the development of Kacchi (raw meat) style—transforming it from a dish for elites into the national favorite of Old Dhaka.

Ingredients

  • Basmati rice
  • Chicken or mutton
  • Onions
  • Yogurt
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Green chilies
  • Biryani masala
  • Saffron
  • Whole spices (cardamom, cloves, cinnamon)
  • Mint and coriander leaves

Steps

  1. Marinate meat with yogurt and spices for 1 hour
  2. Soak basmati rice for 30 minutes
  3. Fry onions until golden brown (beresta)
  4. Cook marinated meat until tender
  5. Parboil rice with whole spices
  6. Layer rice and meat in a heavy pot
  7. Add saffron milk, fried onions, and herbs
  8. Seal pot and cook on low heat (dum) for 25 minutes
  9. Let rest for 10 minutes before serving
